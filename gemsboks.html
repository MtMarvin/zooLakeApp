GEMSBOK

The gemsbok (or gemsbuck), Oryx gazella, is a large antelope in the Oryx genus. It is native to the arid regions of Southern Africa, including the Kalahari Desert. Some authorities previously considered the East African oryx a subspecies of the gemsbok.

The gemsbok is featured on the coat of arms of Namibia, where the population is currently estimated at over 373,000 individuals.

The name "gemsbok" comes from the Afrikaans word, which itself originates from the Dutch name for the male chamois. Despite some visual similarities, particularly in facial markings, the chamois and oryx are not closely related.
